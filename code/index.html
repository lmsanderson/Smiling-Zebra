Hi, I'm Matthew Sanderson. I am a User Experience Developer (aka Front End Developer, aka Interface Developer) - henceforth referred to as a "UX Developer" or UXD.

The reason for the overly complex title explanation? Well, as my role has evolved so has my title, and I think that’s worth a short explanation.

When I began developing web pages with HTML and CSS, my role was - like the languages themselves - much simpler. I was handed a design and I coded it, or if I was feeling particularly inspired, I would do both.

Simple right?

Anyhow, as time went by and the web became an interconnected mess of terrible sites, beautiful sites, and inspiring sites that managed to work great code and design together, so too my role became more complex.

As time went by and the web became an interconnected mess of terrible sites, beautiful sites, and inspiring sites

I became the bridge between the Designer and the Back-End Developer, I wasn't the one designing the interface and I wasn't the one providing all of the back end functionality, but I was (and am) the implementor of the interface that sits on the backend systems, and is the design envisioned.

So it came to be that I wasn&rsqou;s really "just" a Front-End Developer anymore, but really the dictator of the User’s experience of a web site, thus UXD became my title.

Contained within this site you’ll find a variety of blog posts relating to new programming and web technologies, as well as general technology news, as well as social posts and alerts.
